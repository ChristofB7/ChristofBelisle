Project Tentacle

Octopus Escape - Unity Based Game.

  • By Christof Belisle
  • May 19, 2021

Octopus Escape was a fun weekend of game development. Participating in the Brackey's 2020 Game Jam, I had 48 hours to develop an idea around the Theme "Out of Control."

Pixel Art and Game Design

Having spent the summer learning how to do pixel art, and learning how to develop games using unity, this was a perfect chance to show off what I had learned. 48 Hours proved to be the test of my skills and creativity. The pixel art was a big part of the game. Creating the assets, animating and creating levels was a challenge. As my first project using my own home designed art I'm quite proud of the piece.

The idea was that you don't control your character. You give it commands blindly mapping the movement of the octopus through the level without ever moving the character itself. It proved to be hard. The controls were certainly not clear and that became its biggest flaw. People weren't sure how to play the game. After the game jam I tweaked it a bit and made it a little more managable. Further Development

Developing the game further and improving based on feedback gave the game a new life. It became more playable, slower paced, and more managable. It game the game playability that was more than just a few minutes confused sitting there confused on what to do with no chance of trying it on your own. This was a great project, much like Cookwell this was another piece that I was passionate about, that I spent some time away from school to learn how to do.
